DISCONNECTED

"SOMETIMES WE NEED TO DISCONNECT IN ORDER TO RECONNECT WITH WHAT MATTERS."

59.5% of the global population access the Internet

That's 4.66 billion active users worldwide

The world has never been more connected

and yet it has never been more disconnected

Toxic online behaviors have been on the rise

hate speech

sexual harassment

flaming

but...

WHY?

DISSOCIATIVE ANONYMITY

Moving around the internet

the people you encounter

can't easily tell who you are.

People only know what you tell them about yourself.

When people have the opportunity to separate their actions from their real world and identity

they feel less vulnerable about opening up.

Whatever they say

or do

can't be directly linked to the rest of their lives.

They don't have to own their behavior by acknowledging it within the full context of who they really are.

When acting out hostile feelings

the person doesn't have to take responsibility for those actions

they might even convince themselves that those behaviors

aren't me at all.

We become

disconnected.

The roots of internet toxicity stem from dissociative anonymity.

And since the boom of social media

and interacting with strangers online has become a lot more common

moderating such discourse has been a technical and ethical challenge for many platforms.

While freedom of speech is important

creating an inclusive platform for meaningful discourse is also necessary.

Ethically, punishing toxic online behavior contradicts net neutrality

Net Neutrality: the principle that all Internet communications must be treated equally and must not be discriminated against based on user, content, website, platform, etc.

The change should happen among people instead

People should disconnect their cyber-self from their real self

build an emotional and mental barrier

care less about what other people say

and focus on yourself.

Alternatively...

Net neutrality is already being undermined

with internet toxicity preventing equal access.

The bullied are afraid

and become silent.

How could we technologically reconnect

and punish bad online behavior?

MODERATION

Moderation solutions already exist.

Human and bot moderators read millions of messages a day.

When a message is deemed as toxic or inapporpriate

the toxic user is punished

but the current punishments are work-around-able

time-outs are short

bans are short

alternate accounts can be made

The disconnection between the people and the punishments prevent the effects of a punishment

HARDWARE BANNING

A forceful reconnection through hardware banning could do the trick.

Repeated toxic behavior

resulting in a hardware ban

prevents access to the website, platform, or application through the same banned hardware.

The consequences of your actions will be cemented.
